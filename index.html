Research Interest

My research interests are all over the place, but in particular, I had a lot of fun exploring the computational side of astronomy, which includes automation and machine learning which can be applied to any field of study! Some of my favorite projects included applications of Bayesian inference and creating my own convulational neural networks for inference. In future studies, I would like to leverage Machine Learning Models for inference in uncoming Sky Surveys. At this point in time, I am open to all topics under cosmology and high energy astrophysics.

Outreach

Astronomy C10 UGSI

I was an undergraduate graduate student instructor (UGSI) for Alex Filippenko's introduction to general astronomy. This survey course brought together 900 students from various academic backgrounds and engage them in all of astronomy. I also orgnaized the star parties where students were able to view nearby objects on the Treffers Telescope and deep sky objects with the eVscopes on the roof of Campbell Hall. As a UGSI, I lead two discussion sections, created weekly lesson plans, hosted exam review sessions as well as The Astronomy Learning Center. This formal teaching experience was by far the most rewarding and a highlight of my undergraduate experience.

Calculus Round Table

Calculus Roundtable is a nonprofit organization that is working to accelerate math and science skills for students of color. Our vision is to give all students an understanding of the world through the lens of math and science and that their future lives are not decided by random but by effort. We help students gain access to tutoring, STEM afterschool programs to boost their math and science scores.

At Calculus Round Table, I had the privilege of leading Python and Astronomy workshops in K-8 classrooms. The workshops utilized engaging platforms such as Scratch and Code Monkey to introduce students to the world of programming and astronomy. In collaboration with the West Contra Costa Unified School District, I provided one-on-one mentorship to high school students, offering guidance and support in their pursuit of higher education. Moreover, our team had the opportunity to make a positive impact at the Juvenile Justice Center in San Leandro, CA. We worked with enthusiastic students who aspired to pursue higher education despite facing challenges related to their involvement with the justice system.

R.I.S.E Peer Mentor

RISE offers academic support and college preparation programs for low-income At-Promise youth who will be attending Berkeley High Schools. Our goals are to help students graduate on time and encourage them to pursue higher education. We achieve these goals by reducing outside barriers that interfere with personal and academic wellness through Summer Bridge Transition, Academic Mentoring, Counseling, Advocacy and Enrichment Activities.

As a peer mentor in the RISE youth program, I played a vital role in shaping students' academic journeys. Beyond assisting with homework, I provided comprehensive mentorship to guide students towards college readiness. My dedication extended to instilling confidence, imparting essential leadership skills, and fostering a supportive environment.
